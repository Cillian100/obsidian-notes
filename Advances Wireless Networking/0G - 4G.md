## 0G
- Mobile radio telephone systems were mobile telephone systems that proceeded modern cellular technologies. 
## 1G
- 1st generation of mobile telecommunication 
- developed in 1980s and completed in early 1990s
- provides a speed up to 2.4kbps 
- based on analogue systems 
- allows users to make a call in one country
- key issues:
	- low capacity 
	- unreliable hand-off
	- poor voice links
	- no security 

## 2G
- 2nd generation of mobile telecommunication 
- developed in late 1980s and completed in late 1990s 
- based on digital systems 
- provides speed up to 64kbps 

- The most common 2G technology has been the GSM standard which became the first globally adapted for mobile communication. 
	- other 2G technologies include cdmaOne, Digital AMPS, Personal Digital Cellular and Personal Handy-phone System.
	- the transition to digital technology enabled the implementation of encryption for voice calls and data transmission.
	- 2G networks were primarily deigned to support voice calls and SMS, with later advancements enabling always-on packet services, including email and limited internet access.

## 3G
- NTT DoCoMo launched the first 3G network on 1 October 2001, using the WCMDA technologies 
- Bandwidth of 3G networks in 128Kbps for mobile stations, and 2Mbps for fixed applications
- The current trend in mobile systems is to support the high bit rate data services at the downlink via High Speed Downlink Packet Access (HSDPA).
